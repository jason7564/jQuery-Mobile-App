Step 1 of 9

Make sure you have the time and ingredients!

  • Prep Time: 20 Minutes
  • Cook Time: 25 Minutes
  • Total Time: 45 Minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2x 8oz packages of cream cheese
  • 3/4 cup of sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 1 egg
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 12x vanilla wafer cookies
  • canned cherries in syrup

Step 2 of 9

Crush Crush Crush

Crush up all the vanilla wafers like the hopes of the Ottawa Senators ever winning the Stanley Cup and fill the bottom of a pie plate.

Step 3 of 9

Mix

Thoroughly mix the cream cheese with sugar until all the rebel clumps have been assimilated

Step 4 of 9

More Mixing

After all clumps have been assimilated mix in the egg, egg yolk, and the vanilla extract. Ensure that all hidden clump strongholds have been destroyed. Persistant scum.

Step 5 of 9

Intermission

You're more than halfway there now! Take a break, go enjoy a nice cold beer, you've earned it. A weary baker is a bad baker.

Step 6 of 9

Drown The Wafers

Now that you're nice and relaxed, grab your pie plate that you've previously filled in with crushed wafers ("See Step 2 of 9") and pour in your cream cheese mixture. Delicately smoothe out the surface if needed.

Step 7 of 9

Final Stretch

Once your oven is preheated to 350F place the pie plate on the middle rack and let it bake at 350F for 25 minutes. You can use this time to go enjoy another beer, you're an accomplished baker afterall, you deserve the finer things in life.

Step 8 of 9

Cherries!

Now that the 25 minutes are up, remove the pie plate from the oven and let it sit for 5 minutes, or just enough time for you to go mano a mano with that can of cherries. Once you've got your can open drain any excess water that may be inside, then scoop the rest out on top of the cake and spread them evenly.

Step 9 of 9

Congratulations!

You've done it! You've baked the perfect cheesecake! Go ahead, indulge, but tread carefully. The dark arts of cheesecakery are not to be abused.
